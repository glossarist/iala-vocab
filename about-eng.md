# About the IALA Dictionary

## Overview

The IALA Dictionary is a comprehensive multilingual reference for terminology used in marine aids to navigation. Developed and maintained by the International Association of Marine Aids to Navigation and Lighthouse Authorities (IALA), this dictionary serves professionals, maritime authorities, and organizations working to harmonize navigation systems worldwide. The IALA Dictionary provides standardized terminology across 13 specialized sections, ensuring consistent communication and understanding among maritime stakeholders globally.

## The International Association of Marine Aids to Navigation and Lighthouse Authorities

IALA is an intergovernmental organization dedicated to promoting the harmonization and standardization of marine aids to navigation systems. As a leading authority in maritime safety and navigation, IALA develops guidelines, recommendations, and technical standards that are adopted by maritime nations and organizations worldwide. The IALA Dictionary reflects decades of expertise and international collaboration in defining the precise terminology essential for safe and efficient maritime navigation.

## Dictionary Editions

### 1970–1989 Edition

The original IALA Dictionary was compiled over two decades, from 1970 to 1989, representing the foundational work of international maritime experts. This comprehensive edition established the baseline terminology for marine aids to navigation, covering essential concepts and definitions that have remained relevant for decades. The 1970–1989 edition serves as a historical reference and demonstrates the evolution of maritime terminology during a critical period of standardization.

### 2023 Revision

The 2023 Revision of the IALA Dictionary represents a modern update to the original work, incorporating contemporary developments in maritime technology, navigation systems, and international standards. This revised edition reflects current practices in electronic navigation, vessel traffic services, automatic identification systems, and emerging technologies in maritime safety. The 2023 Revision ensures that the IALA Dictionary remains a current and authoritative resource for maritime professionals.

## About This Site

This vocabulary browser provides an interactive interface for exploring the IALA Dictionary across both editions. Users can search, browse, and compare terminology across the 13 specialized sections: Overview, General Terms, Visual Aids, Audible Aids, Radio Aids, RCM & Reliability, Power Supplies, Civil Engineering, Floating Equipment, VTS, e-Navigation, AIS, and Heritage. The browser supports multiple languages and facilitates cross-referencing between editions.

## Authority and Source

The IALA Dictionary is the official terminology resource of the International Association of Marine Aids to Navigation and Lighthouse Authorities. For the authoritative source and additional information, visit the [IALA Dictionary](https://www.iala.int/wiki/dictionary/) on the official IALA website.
